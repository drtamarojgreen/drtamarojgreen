## About Me

I believe software engineering is more than writing code—it is the discipline of transforming complex ideas into reliable, understandable systems.

My interests span autonomous AI agents, computational neuroscience, cognitive modeling, software architecture, scientific visualization, and scalable engineering. Whether I am modeling neural communication, designing programming languages for intelligent systems, or building workflow automation frameworks, I strive to create software that is modular, explainable, and grounded in sound engineering principles.

Many of my projects are interconnected components of a larger research vision exploring how biological intelligence and artificial intelligence can inform one another.

## 🔭 I'm currently working on...

A growing ecosystem of research-driven software projects focused on advancing explainable artificial intelligence and computational neuroscience.

Current initiatives include:

- **PrismQuanta** — a modular AI platform for autonomous reasoning, workflow orchestration, and local LLM integration.
- **Quanta Tissu** — a computational framework for modeling and processing large languages.
- **TissLang** — a behavioral pseudo-language designed to improve structured communication between humans and large language models.
- **Computational Brain Modeling** — lightweight simulations of neurons, neurotransmitters, hormones, glial cells, and interconnected brain regions.
- **AI Visualization Tools** — generating SVG, 3D models, and scientific visualizations that make complex systems easier to understand.

Every project contributes toward a long-term goal of developing transparent, explainable, and scientifically grounded intelligent systems.
